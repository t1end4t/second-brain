Phase Details

Phase 1 — Ingest

Drop paper → Register in vault

Trigger: You drop a PDF into raw/ICLR 2026/ (or any subfolder)
  • Record the paper in _sources.md with filename, type, date, title from filename
  • No summary yet — that comes from your external tool
  • Appends to _sources.md — never overwrites
Phase 2 — Read

External tool does the heavy lifting

Trigger: You have a new paper ready to read
  • Upload the paper to NotebookLLM / your preferred tool
  • Get the AI's analysis (summary, key ideas, methodology, limitations, connections)
  • Copy key findings into the _sources.md entry for that paper
Why this matters: You bring the human-in-the-loop judgment about what's worth capturing. The tool is the reader; you are the curator.
Phase 3 — Synthesize

Turn papers into wiki knowledge

Trigger: You have N papers with summaries and want to organize them
  • Scan _sources.md for entries that lack wiki links
  • Generate concept articles for recurring themes (e.g., "diffusion LLMs", "RL for LLMs")
  • Create topic articles if multiple papers share a theme
  • Update _index.md with new concepts and articles
  • Update _sources.md entries with wiki links
Phase 4 — Query

Ask the vault questions

Trigger: You want to know what the vault knows about a topic
  • Search _sources.md + wiki articles for relevant content
  • Answer against the knowledge base
  • File useful outputs to wiki/articles/
Phase 5 — Maintain

Keep things clean

Trigger: Periodic health check or you notice stale entries
  • Check for orphaned sources (no wiki links, no summaries)
  • Check for duplicate concepts
  • Flag articles that are too thin
  • Suggest consolidations

What Gets Built

kb-ingest
Registers new PDFs, extracts metadata, appends to _sources.md
kb-compile
Generates concept/articles from sources, updates links and index
kb-answer
Answers questions against the wiki + sources
kb-health
Lints and maintains the wiki — flags orphans, duplicates, thin articles
