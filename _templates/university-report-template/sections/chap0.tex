\section{Introduction}

Nonlinear control systems play a significant role in modern engineering applications such as robotics, electromechanical systems, and renewable energy conversion. Among benchmark nonlinear systems, the inverted pendulum is widely recognized as a representative platform due to its inherent instability, nonlinear dynamics, and underactuated characteristics, making it suitable for evaluating advanced control strategies~\cite{ref1,ref2}. Intelligent control methods, particularly fuzzy logic--based approaches, have demonstrated strong robustness and real-time performance in swing-up and stabilization problems compared to conventional linear controllers~\cite{ref1,ref3}. Hybrid fuzzy--PID schemes further improve transient and steady-state responses in practical regulation tasks such as level control under nonlinear and uncertain conditions. In addition, Iterative Learning Control (ILC) has emerged as an effective technique for enhancing tracking accuracy in repetitive and discrete-time systems by utilizing information from previous iterations to reduce tracking errors progressively~\cite{ref3}. The successful application of fuzzy logic in renewable energy systems, including maximum power point tracking for small-scale wind turbines, also highlights its adaptability and effectiveness in nonlinear environments~\cite{ref5}. Motivated by these developments, this study investigates the design of fuzzy logic--based controllers for inverted pendulum balancing, level regulation at specified operating points, and iterative learning control of linear discrete-time systems to improve stability, tracking performance, and convergence characteristics.
