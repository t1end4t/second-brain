# Building as Research

**Status:** Active
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-11

---

## The Distinction

There is a real difference between:
- **Building systems** (implementation)
- **Developing scientific taste** (research)

Both matter. But they train different muscles.

## The Risk

Staying only in implementation risks becoming:
> "an AI systems integrator who reads papers"

Instead of:
> "a researcher who can create new conceptual leverage"

Different trajectory.

## The Shift: Task-Driven → Hypothesis-Driven

### Task-driven building
> "Implement feature."

### Hypothesis-driven building
> "Test whether this interaction pattern improves researcher trust."
> "Test whether memory compression helps multi-session reasoning."
> "Test whether X design reduces hallucination detection latency."

This turns engineering into research. Very important shift.

## The New Learning Loop

Learn → Build → Fail → Debug → Reflect

LLMs dramatically reduce friction for building.
That means: the winners are people who ship more experiments.

Just consuming AI explanations feels productive but isn't.

## Connection to Research

Building keeps ideas grounded in reality.
Without it, ideas become fantasy.

But building without hypothesis-driven thinking is just engineering work — valuable, but not research.

The bridge:
- Frame builds as experiments
- Measure what matters
- Reflect on what happened
- Generalize to concepts
