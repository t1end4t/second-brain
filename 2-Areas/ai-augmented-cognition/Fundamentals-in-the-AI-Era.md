# Fundamentals in the AI Era

**Status:** Active
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-11

---

## The Shift

Before AI: knowledge = advantage
Now: judgment + taste + problem framing + execution = advantage

LLMs generate information instantly. They still struggle with:
- Deciding what matters
- Deep understanding
- Long-term strategy
- Verifying truth
- Creativity with constraints
- Connecting ideas across domains

## What Still Compounds

### Math (intuition, not theorems)

Not advanced theorem proving. More likely:
- Probability intuition
- Optimization intuition
- Statistics
- Information theory mindset
- Linear algebra fluency

Modern AI papers hide simple ideas under dense notation. Math intuition cuts through that.

### Programming (systems, not frameworks)

Not "more frameworks." Instead:
- Systems thinking
- Concurrency
- Memory
- Distributed systems
- Tooling literacy
- Reading large codebases

Strongest AI researchers often have very strong systems intuition.

### Writing (argument construction)

Scientific writing. Technical writing. Argument construction.

If you can think clearly AND write clearly, you become extremely dangerous intellectually.

### One Underrated Fundamental: Taste

The ability to distinguish:
- Meaningful from trivial
- Elegant from hacky
- Real novelty from rebranding
- Signal from noise

Developed through: exposure + comparison + reflection + building.

## Memorization Is NOT Dead

You still need:
- Mental models
- Vocabulary
- Core facts
- Intuition

Otherwise you can't detect AI mistakes. Plausible nonsense passes through.

The ideal balance:
- Memorize foundations
- Outsource lookup
- Internalize concepts

## The Beginner's Trap

Beginners who rely too early on AI become dependent.
Strong fundamentals still compound massively.

Use AI like:
- Tutor
- Debugger
- Simulator
- Sparring partner

Not like:
- Copy-paste machine
