# Socratic AI Learning

**Status:** Active
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-11

---

## The Pattern

Traditional AI use:
> "Explain this chapter." → passive consumption → illusion of learning

Socratic AI use:
> "Quiz me one step at a time." → recall + struggle + correction → real learning

Learning happens during:
- Recall (not recognition)
- Struggle (not comfort)
- Correction (not confirmation)

Not during passive reading or reading AI summaries.

## Effective Prompts (Examples)

```
"Quiz me one step at a time. Don't give the answer immediately."
"Challenge my assumptions about this topic."
"Find flaws in my reasoning."
"Ask me questions first, then let me answer from memory."
"What am I missing in this argument?"
"Stress-test this idea."
```

## The Loop

1. Study / think independently first
2. Ask AI to quiz or challenge you
3. Answer from memory
4. Check against AI feedback
5. Save weak spots for spaced review

## Research Support

Socratic-style questioning improves critical thinking better than direct-answer tutoring systems.

See: [[Cognitive-Modes-and-Rhythm]] for how this fits into daily rhythm.

## Bad AI Learning Habits

- Summarizing everything instead of thinking
- Generating full assignments
- Copy-paste answers
- Endless prompting without building
- Reading AI output as a replacement for reading source material

## Good AI Learning Habits

- Tutoring and Socratic dialogue
- Debugging and error explanation
- Simulations and thought experiments
- Brainstorming and critique
- Deliberate practice partner
- Acceleration (not replacement)
