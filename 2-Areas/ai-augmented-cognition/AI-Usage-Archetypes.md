# AI Usage Archetypes

**Status:** Active
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-11

---

## Overview

Different people use LLMs at work in fundamentally different ways. These are not rigid categories — most people shift between them. But recognizing which mode you're in helps you choose better.

## The Five Archetypes

### 1. The Copier

**Pattern:** Dump task → copy output → light edit → ship

**Works for:** Routine emails, basic code, summaries, repetitive admin

**Danger:** Thinking muscles atrophy. Output looks fine until it suddenly isn't. Stops noticing subtle mistakes.

**Short-term:** Productive
**Long-term:** Degraded judgment

---

### 2. The Delegator

**Pattern:** Treat AI like junior employee → break work into chunks → review critically → redirect → maintain final judgment

**Strength:** Becomes absurdly productive. Turns from craftsman into editor-director.

**Key skill:** Critical review + redirection + judgment maintenance

---

### 3. The Conversational Thinker

**Pattern:** Use AI as cognitive whiteboard → argue, stress-test, find hidden assumptions → sharpen thinking through dialogue

**Output:** Most original work, because judgment is being sharpened, not outsourced.

**Metaphor:** Jazz improvisation partner. Duck on the river. Mirror maze for thought.

**Key prompts:**
- "Argue against this idea."
- "What am I missing?"
- "Why does this feel wrong?"
- "Find hidden assumptions."

---

### 4. The Vibe Operator

**Pattern:** Build things they don't fully understand → prototypes, internal tools, experiments

**Strength:** Low friction → high experiment velocity. Sometimes creates real innovation through playfulness.

**Danger:** When complexity scales, debugging matters, security matters — the floor disappears. Vibes were standing on plywood.

**Not dismissible:** Playfulness can be a genuine creative force.

---

### 5. The Skeptic

**Pattern:** Refuses AI for certain kinds of thinking

**Valid concerns:** Friction itself can be valuable for deep writing, system design, learning math, strategy, philosophy. Removing all friction removes memory formation and struggle-based insight.

**Respectable:** Some people intentionally preserve parts of their mind like a protected forest.

---

## The Emerging Pattern

The strongest workers are **fluid**:

- They know when to think alone
- They know when to collaborate with AI
- They know when to verify
- They know when speed matters more than elegance (and vice versa)

This balance is becoming a real professional skill.

## The Deeper Shift

Before: intelligence = producing answers
Now: intelligence = directing systems effectively

The human role moves upward:
- Less manual execution
- More architecture
- More orchestration
- More taste

## A Hard Truth

The people who are best with AI are often people who were already good without AI.

AI multiplies effort. It doesn't replace the need to build real capacity first.
